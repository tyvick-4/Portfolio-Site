## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Discord has a $248K–$279K problem: 99.9% of its content is invisible to search engines and AI models, while competitor Reddit rode open content to the #3 US website, $1.3B revenue, and the #1 AI citation ranking. Discord's 400M+ dormant registered users represent the largest untapped reactivation pool in consumer social. The Staff PM, Growth & SEO role exists to build the "selective openness" architecture that unlocks organic search as a top-of-funnel acquisition and dormant user reactivation channel — without compromising Discord's privacy-first identity.

This document synthesizes research across three dimensions: (1) the competitive SEO/AEO landscape, (2) retention and reactivation mechanics, and (3) direct user voice data explaining why different segments leave and return. It's designed to be passed to AI tools for interview practice.

## SECTION 1: THE COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE — DISCORD vs. REDDIT vs. THE OPEN WEB

### 1.1 Discord's Invisibility Problem

| Dimension | Discord | Reddit |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Daily messages | 4B+ | 550M+ posts/year |
| Content indexed by Google | ~0% (robots.txt blocks /channels/) | ~100% of public content |
| Publicly discoverable servers | ~32,000 of 28.4M (0.11%) | 3.4M+ subreddits, 100K+ active |
| Google search visibility rank | Not ranked | #3 in US (from #78 in 2023) |
| AI citation share | 0% | #1 most-cited domain globally |
| Organic search traffic share | Negligible | 68.2% of all Reddit traffic |
| Data licensing revenue | $0 | $203M+ in disclosed contracts |

**The technical block**: Discord's `robots.txt` contains `Disallow: /channels/` which blocks 100% of message content. Forum Channels — structurally identical to traditional indexable forums — are also blocked. Server Web Pages remain in limited beta.

**Third-party evidence of unmet demand**: AnswerOverflow, Sift.ai, and Linen emerged specifically to bridge Discord content to the web. Their existence proves the market wants this solved.

### 1.2 Reddit's SEO Explosion — The Playbook Discord Must Study

**Timeline of Reddit's search dominance:**
- July 2023: Reddit ranked #78 in US search visibility
- Feb 2024: $60M/year Google deal + IPO filing
- April 2024: Organic visits surge from 57M to 427M/month
- July 2024: Climbs to #5 most visible domain (1,328% increase)
- Q3 2024: Logged-out daily users grow 70% YoY
- Q1 2025: 108.1M DAUq, +31% YoY
- Q2 2025: Logged-out users become 55.4% of all daily traffic
- 2025: 68.2% of all Reddit traffic from organic search
- 2025: Reddit ranks for 595M+ keywords in Google

**What drove it:**
1. Google-Reddit deal gave Google real-time API access; Google favored Reddit in SERPs
2. Reddit content naturally answers questions (Q&A format aligns with search intent)
3. Google's Helpful Content Update (2022-2024) prioritized first-person UGC over corporate content
4. "Discussions and forums" SERP features prominently highlight Reddit
5. 3.4M+ subreddits provide massive long-tail keyword coverage

**Reddit's vulnerability (Discord's opening):**
- Q4 2024: Google algorithm change caused user growth miss, 15%+ stock drop
- Logged-out users are low-value "flyby visitors" with minimal engagement
- Reddit sued Perplexity for unauthorized scraping — tension between openness and control
- Q1 2025 algorithm shifts deprioritized some UGC content
- Discord could build a HIGHER-QUALITY search experience: curated Forum content vs. Reddit's everything-is-public noise

### 1.3 The AI Citation Economy

| Platform | Perplexity Citation Share | Google AI Overview Share | ChatGPT Share |
|----------|--------------------------|------------------------|---------------|
| Reddit | 46.7% of top-10 citations | 21.0% (largest source) | 11.3% |
| Discord | 0% | 0% | 0% |

**Key statistics shaping this landscape:**
- 60-69% of Google searches now result in zero clicks (up from 56% in 2024)
- AI Overviews appear on 18-20% of searches
- Gartner: 25% of search shifts to answer engines by 2026
- ChatGPT serves 800M users weekly
- Semrush: Average AI search visitor is 4.4x as valuable as average organic visitor

**The compounding invisibility problem**: Content on the open web gets cited by AI → builds authority → gets cited more. Discord content generates zero citations, builds zero AI authority, compounds to nothing.

### 2.4 Search as a Reactivation Lever — The Core Thesis

**Traditional reactivation (push-based):** Discord sends email/push → User has already muted notifications → Message ignored → User stays dormant

**Search-driven reactivation (pull-based):** Dormant user searches Google for something → Finds Discord community content → Recognizes their community → Returns on their own terms

**Reddit proves this works at massive scale:**
- Logged-out daily users grew 70% YoY in Q3 2024
- By Q2 2025, logged-out visitors = 55.4% of all daily traffic
- 68.2% of Reddit's total traffic from organic search
- International logged-out growth: +54% YoY (vs. +22% US)

**The reactivation loop for Discord:**
```
Dormant user searches "best Elden Ring DLC builds"
  → Discord forum post appears in Google results
  → User recognizes their community, sees recent discussions
  → CTA: "Join the conversation — 2,400 people discussing this now"
  → User logs back into Discord → Reactivated
```

**Conservative quantification:**
- 400M dormant registered users
- If 5% touched by search quarterly = 20M potential reactivations
- At industry 15% resurrection rate = 3M reactivated users/quarter
- At 12% DAU/MAU conversion = 360K additional DAU
- Compounds: each reactivated user makes their community more valuable for other dormant members

## SECTION 3: USER VOICE — WHY PEOPLE LEAVE AND RETURN

### 3.1 Five User Personas

**Persona 1: The Gamer (74% of servers)**
- Goes dormant when game lifecycle ends; activity directly tied to releases
- Returns when new games launch and friend groups reassemble
- Server fatigue: visits avg 7 servers/day, notification volume becomes noise between games
- 93% of Discord users game on platform; 2B hours of gaming/month
- 90% of activity in small servers (avg 4 people per voice call)
- Search solution: Index game community forums so search re-engages between releases

**Persona 2: The Server Admin / Community Architect**
- Moderator burnout is Discord's most documented churn driver for this segment
- Discord itself published extensive burnout resources, partnered with TWLOHA
- Discord's own docs: "Your account may be in the server and your name on the member list, but you're no longer an active community member"
- AutoMod V2 reduced manual intervention 37% but doesn't solve emotional toll
- Search solution: Give admins growth metrics from search — seeing their content reach 50K people via Google reignites motivation

**Persona 3: The Non-Gamer / Community Seeker (growing to 55%+ by 2026)**
- "Wall of Text Fatigue": 50 channels, hundreds of roles, constant pings → Mute → Forget → Churn
- Discovery Deficit: "Discord feels like a dark room" without direct invite links
- Ephemeral value: knowledge scrolls away unlike persistent forum content
- 46% of active users are non-gamers; mental health groups grew 31% YoY
- Non-gaming engagement outpaces gaming by 8%
- Search solution: HIGHEST potential — make community content discoverable for education, creative, study groups

**Persona 4: The Privacy-Conscious User**
- Age verification backlash (Feb 2026): Discord expects "some sort of hit" to user numbers
- AI feature resistance: Jan 2026 survey on voice recording/summarization met with overwhelmingly negative feedback
- Data collection concerns documented in detailed blog posts
- Network lock-in keeps them: "no better application out there"
- Search solution: Opt-in indexing demonstrates user control, builds trust

**Persona 5: The International User (34% APAC, fastest growing)**
- Language barriers, timezone misalignment, regional alternatives
- Reddit's international logged-out growth: 54% YoY (vs. 22% US)
- Search solution: Multi-language forum indexing; international search-driven discovery is fastest-growing vector

### 3.2 Cross-Cutting Themes from User Feedback

**Theme 1: "Discord Is a Knowledge Black Hole"** (most relevant to role)
- Slashdot: "Discord is great for real time chats but more of an ephemeral and transitory chat site"
- Blog (Feb 2026): "You have to join the Discord, jump through hoops... it's probably a question that's been answered somewhere in chat that nobody can find"
- Forum nostalgia: "Everything had a forum, and it was actually really well-organized"
- Game dev Dan Salvato: "An admin feature to mirror the server to a public web archive would benefit humanity"

**Theme 2: Notification Overload → Mute → Forget → Churn**
- User joins → overwhelmed by notifications → mutes everything → forgets → dormant
- Only re-entry path is active notification — which they already muted
- Search creates passive re-entry paths that don't require push notifications

**Theme 3: UI Changes Drive Acute Churn Events**
- Mobile UI update generated intense backlash: "2016 user here! this app has been uninstalled"
- Creates pool of recently dormant users with high resurrection potential
- These users didn't lose interest — they left over fixable frustration

**Theme 4: Network Lock-In = High Resurrectibility**
- "It only makes sense to use a service that other people have already adopted"
- 88% of US users say they'd likely use Discord again
- Users haven't psychologically churned — they need a trigger. Search provides ambient triggers.

## SECTION 4: STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK FOR THE INTERVIEW

### 4.1 The "Selective Openness" Architecture

Not a binary choice between open (Reddit) and closed (current Discord). The opportunity is designing opt-in layers:

| Content Layer | Current State | Proposed State | Rationale |
|--------------|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| Real-time chat | Private | Private | Core value proposition |
| Forum Channels | Blocked by robots.txt | Indexable (admin opt-in) | Structured like forums, lowest-hanging fruit |
| Server descriptions | Thin metadata | Rich, SEO-optimized pages | Discovery entry point |
| Pinned resources | Private | Optionally public | High-value persistent content |
| Event pages | Private | Optionally public | Time-sensitive discovery |
| Voice/video | Private | Private | Privacy essential |

### 4.2 Phased Roadmap

**Phase 1 (0-6 months): Foundation**
- Audit and optimize existing web presence (server pages, invite pages, marketing)
- Design opt-in framework for Forum Channel indexing
- Build experimentation infrastructure for landing page A/B testing
- Establish baseline metrics (search impressions, CTR, conversion)

**Phase 2 (6-12 months): Scale**
- Launch indexable Forum Channels for opted-in servers
- Build programmatic SEO landing pages at scale for gaming communities
- Implement structured data / schema markup
- A/B test landing page experiences for logged-out users

**Phase 3 (12-18 months): GEO & Expansion**
- Optimize for AI citation (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity)
- Explore data licensing relationships (similar to Reddit-Google deal)
- Expand to non-gaming verticals (education, creators, study groups)
- Launch search-driven resurrection campaigns for dormant users

### 4.3 Metrics Framework

**North Star**: Organic search impressions leading to meaningful engagement on Discord

**Acquisition Metrics:**
- Organic search impressions on Discord web properties
- CTR from search to Discord pages
- New user registrations from search landing pages
- Server join rate from organic search visitors

**Reactivation Metrics:**
- Resurrection rate: % of dormant users (60+ days inactive) who return
- Search-driven resurrection rate: % reactivated via search entry points
- Time-to-reactivation from first search touchpoint
- Reactivated user D7/D30 retention vs. new user retention

**Engagement Metrics:**
- DAU/MAU ratio improvement from 12% baseline
- Search-referred user session depth vs. direct/invite-referred
- Server discovery rate from search

**GEO/AEO Metrics:**
- Discord citation frequency across AI answer engines
- Share of gaming-related AI answers referencing Discord communities
- Branded search volume growth ("discord [game name]" queries)
